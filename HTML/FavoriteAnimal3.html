Labrador Duck

Camptorhynchus labradorius

This picture comes from the South Dakota Birds and Birding website.

This picture comes from Wikipedia.

This picture comes from Montgomery College that was in an exhibit.

The Labrador Ducks only ate small molluscs, which were located in the ocean. Since the molluscs have hard shells, the labrador duck had a strong, modified beak to get into the shell. The structure of their beak was a wide, flattened tip with many lamellae in their mouth which would filter out bugs, sand, or other food items.
